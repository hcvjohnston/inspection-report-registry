    How this estimate is calculated

    We start from HomeGuide's 2026 cost-by-square-footage table (e.g. $200–$250 under 1,000 sq ft, $300–$400 for 1,501–2,000 sq ft, $450–$500+ for 2,501–3,000 sq ft), adjust it by your state's published average range relative to the $200–$500 national range, add $100–$200 for homes built before 1980 (older homes take longer to inspect and may involve asbestos- or lead-era materials), and add the published price range of each specialty inspection you select. Homes over 3,000 sq ft are extrapolated at roughly $25 per additional 500 sq ft. Condo and mobile-home pricing uses HomeGuide's property-type figures. HomeAdvisor's national data (average $343; most homeowners pay $296–$424) is a useful cross-check on any result.

    Frequently asked questions

    How much does a home inspection cost in 2026?
    Most standard inspections run $200–$500, with national averages around $340 (HomeGuide) to $343 (HomeAdvisor; most homeowners pay $296–$424).

    Who pays for the inspection?
    The buyer typically hires, schedules, and pays for it shortly after an offer is accepted. Sellers occasionally pay for a pre-listing inspection.

    Is a home inspection required?
    Not by law, but lenders or insurers sometimes require one, and skipping it can leave you responsible for expensive undiscovered problems.

    How long does an inspection take?
    Usually 2–4 hours, with the written report delivered within about 1–3 days.
